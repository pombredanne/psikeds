Licensing the psikeds

The ps induced knowledge entity delivery system is intended to be usable without (nearly) any restrictions - even in commercial contexts. Therefore, psiKeds wants to be free software: On the one hand, the developers want to assure that the psiKeds kernel (server:server) is free software and stays free software for ever and for each user. On the other hand, psiKeds wants to enable third parties to develop and distribute additional tools, simplifying supplements or improving replacements (if necessary also under other licenses and even if the psiKeds authors themselves prefer to develop and use free and open source software). Therefore, the psiKeds components are licensed under different (open source) licenses:

Specification:

The psiKeds:server:server and all its newly developed libraries, modules, plugins, and scripts are licensed under the AGPL v3.0: whoever is interacting with any psiKeds instance remotely through a computer network must also get an opportunity to receive the corresponding sources of this (modified) running version.

The psiKeds:client:server as whole is licensed under the GPL v.3.0. Whoever distributes a (modified) version of this component must also distribute the corresponding sources under the GPL v.3.0. But you are allowed to develop your own client (which then must not be a derivative work of the original psiKeds:client:server)

All newly developed libraries which are constituting the psikeds:client:sever - especially the requestor and the presentor libraries - are licensed under the LGPL v.3.0. Whoever distributes a (modified) version of these libraries must also distribute the corresponding sources of the libraries under the LGPL v.3.0. But you are allowed to develop and distribute your own client under your own terms as an on-top development based on these libraries - if you distribute the libraries themselves under the conditions of the LGPL v3.0.

The psiKeds XSD is licensed under the creatice commons share alike 3.0 license.

The psiKeds knowledge bases belong to their knowledge engineers. Initially, they are not covered by any of the free and or open source licenses mentioned here. But, of course, they can be published as free documents, too, although there is no obligation to do so.

The client applications running on the machines of the requesting users - like Firefox etc. - are licensed under their normal licenses - like the MPL. Also, the systemic prerequisites for the psikeds:server:server and the psikeds:client:server - like a GNU/Linux system or an Apache Tomcat as its web application server - use their own licenses. psiKeds does not want to influence these licensings and is explicitly asking the psiKeds users also to respect these other license constraints.

The knowledge base editors can be licensed under any license. The editor(s) generated by the psiKeds project will generally be published as open source software.

Contributors to the psiKeds project have to sign a psiKeds specific contributor license agreement which shall protect the contributors and the users against unreasonable consequences.
